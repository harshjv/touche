Optical multitouch table

An optical multitouch table uses a camera to track optical indicators that correspond to touch events on the table. They are usually used in conjunction with a rear-projection screen. Most often, this "indicator" is dispersed infrared light.

There are different types of optical multitouch tables, such as DI tables (Diffuse Illumination), FTIR tables and laser-based tables.

There are lots of resources on the internet about how to build such multitouch tables. For a good trade-off between ease-to-build and adequate tracking results, using an FTIR multitouch table is recommended, even though you can also achieve awesome results (though slightly harder to build and to obtain parts for) with laser-based tables.

Touché supports optical multitouch tables that either track bright touches in front of a dark background, or those that track dark touches in front of a bright background.
